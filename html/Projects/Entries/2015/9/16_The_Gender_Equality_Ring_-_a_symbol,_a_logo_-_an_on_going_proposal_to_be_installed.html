The Gender Equality Ring  - a symbol, a logo, an installation - an on going proposal












A collaborative project designed by Yael Reisner and Abel Maciel, (architect & computer engineer)

with Quim Guixa (Engineer, a world expert in making / manufacturing /installing inflatables).

See:    abelmaicel.com    See:    quimguixa.com 


The Concept - All people are equally entitled to human rights without discrimination, and as stated in Article 1 of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights: “All human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights”. We should be all strived to gender equality, and that special ring would be a great gesture, a refreshed icon and a trigger to think and act about it.

The Gender Equality Ring is a fresh, novel and optimistic symbol for gender equality.

Its roll as a large installed inflatable is:

•To enable people to see it from a distance, to congregate under it - standing or lying down under the ring and its oculus - or just pass by.

•To remind the importance of it, to discuss and debate equality of men and women as the basic human right, and to celebrate the equal activity of men and women worldwide where it does happen.

Our proposition combines the well-known gender’s two symbols, enhanced graphically by the spectrum colours symbolising a wide diversity of personalities amongst men and women.

The electromagnetic radiation of all the frequencies in the visible range of the spectrum appears white to the eye. Borrowed from the association with white light that is actually made up of different colours, all added together, (as we can observe when a prism bend white light - refraction - and split it up to its many colours it consists of), this white ring – signifies the merging forces into one coherent collaborative activity: a symbol of equal input into the world of actions and thoughts, as part of a daily pursuit.

Moreover, the symbols of gender operate as the landmark of the ring but also become the structural strategy holding the upper ring.

Below the white ring, a contemplation space is created, portraying aspects of life and active discussions. This is the foundation of the object, also operating as a resting space for visitors.

On the oculus of the ring, interfacing the land and the skies, a surface operates as the mirror of the collective self, presenting the observer as observed. This augmented reality proposes a dynamic poetry and juxtaposition of sky, earth and us. This large imagery is revealed as one approaches the passage. It can also function as the display of information about relevant events.

One can see in the images displayed, two versions, one anchored in an open park and the other in an urban context, each raise to a different height. 


(An on going proposal to be installed as an on going project, that can easily packed, travel, re-located and re-installed.)

The spatial design consists of two elements made of metal sheets: ‘scroll-wall’ and a ‘wing’, which together create the design choreography, and the surfaces for the artist’s work.


Stills from two large epic photography by Ori Gersht, are embedded in the ‘scroll-wall’ and another on both sides of the ‘wing’, creating four large frescos, embossed, pigmented, and perforated into the metal surfaces.




Technical description - The passage is an inflatable structure, made of a flame-retarded textile, filled with air by a low noise electric pump within the object. The structure is anchored by 4mm cables made inconspicuous by black matte finish.

The membrane of the inflatable is finished with pigment based, UV resistant and water/ humidity resistant painting sprayed directly on the surface.

As overall lighting strategy, low intensity searchlights in the designed landscape highlight the features of the inflatable structure. Furthermore, LEDs are installed within the white ring to display it as light source. For the oculus screen, a reflective net is applied horizontally within the diameter of the ring. Imagery and animations are projected from landscape level by short-throw projectors. Hence people will be able to walk or lie down under the net and watch films, real-time footage and data streaming in.

The inflatable span is 35 meters. The inflatable's overall height had two variations; the park variation is higher than the urban one.  ( 10 meters overall height vs. 7 meters.) The white ring's diameter is 20 meters.
