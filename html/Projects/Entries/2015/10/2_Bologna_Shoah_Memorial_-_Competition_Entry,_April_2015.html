Bologna Shoah Memorial - Competition Entry, April 2015












Yael Reisner in collaboration with:

Abel Maciel, architect / computer engineer.

Nick Cramp, environmental engineer, partner @ Max Fordham.

Ori Gersht, fine art photographer.

The memorial square is looking above a site with numerous rail tracks all the way to the horizon. One cannot help but to bring the association with the railway-tracks that led the deportations throughout Europe by the Nazi’s Reichsbhan and the destination to concentration and death camps. Auschwitz was one of them.


The very characteristics of the site’s location, led us to the main ideas, while a concept of duality prevails through the design. The embedded artwork – photography by Ori Gersht - aims at adding to the content and its aesthetics, provoking thoughts and emotions in the beholder.


The views from the memorial square, and the epic photography in it, are very different from the views of the installation from outside the square, where the passer-by will see different imagery.

The orientation in relation to West and East, affects the images due to a unique application of a computational technology.


We aimed at emphasising the dual relationship of the memories of the tragic history vs. thoughts of an emphatic future, the horror lived by the victims versus momentarily pleasure drawn from beauty in nature. We designed the dividing surfaces so the sunrise light would enhance certain views and atmosphere in the square vs. others in sunset time; the two extreme conditions along the daily light changes as the sun moves through the sky.  Similarly, light interaction will take place at nighttime with artificial light.

The back side of the Bologna Shoah Memorial installation, seen from the bridge along Via Matteotti, is embedded with a fresco of colourful abstracted flowers -blooming -  (selected stills from Ori Gersht work - On Reflection) - so as not to impose the memorial’s experience on the daily commuters outside of the memorial square. (See originals further below.)

The spatial design consists of two elements made of metal sheets: ‘scroll-wall’ and a ‘wing’, which together create the design choreography, and the surfaces for the artist’s work.


Stills from two large epic photography by Ori Gersht, are embedded in the ‘scroll-wall’ and another on both sides of the ‘wing’, creating four large frescos, embossed, pigmented, and perforated into the metal surfaces.


The encoded ‘scroll-wall’ separates diagonally the memorial square into two parts, the opened side (further secluded from the main street by seven local Amarena cherry trees) and the enclosed one, both exposed to the environment’s elements. The trees are grown in large pots that also serve as public seating.  The interior space is further enclosed by the encoded 'wing'-wall.


We have two juxtaposed layers, along the ‘scroll-wall’ and the ‘wing’, creating a different content for each side - a dual effect per wall using our proprietary CNC manufacturing script. For example, during morning / day time, one can see along the front of the scroll–wall, the embossed deportation scenes heightened, but as the sun sets,Gersht photography of ‘Judith’ in between the deportees’ archival images, become more evident as the sunlight shines through the perforation. (See in the short film below, which was submitted with the competition entry.) These perforations are always executed in an angle, therefore depending where one is in the square, the scroll-wall’s specific faces are seen more intensely than others. Moreover, due to the angular perforation of Judith’s faces, as one walks around the wall and enter the enclosed space,  passing by the 2nd large photo by Gersht - Floating Petals-Black Water- Judith would still follow the beholder like a ghost. (See render.) Each side of the wall is a large fresco.


In a similar method and technology applied, though not similar in content, two juxtaposed photographic epics, were embedded in the ‘wing’ wall, after being selected from the same work - On Reflection. Two very different fresco: Shuttered Mirror and Blooming.

Shuttered Mirror is the fresco embedded on the interior surface portrays a scene of shuttered domestic world and great sadness, where as the exterior surface of the wing, seen from the bridge along Via Matteotti, and Bologna station as well,(see renders),  communicates colorful abstracted flowers -Blooming. 

These display choices are there so as not to impose the memorial’s experience on the daily commuters out of the square, but enhance the surroundings with the message of beauty and hope, adding qualities to the urban context.







Sunset - Front view at the Bologna Shoah Memorial square.

The design pursuit was to explore ways to include imagery in architecture in a fresh way.

The ambition was - after selecting 2D work by the artist we collaborate with -  to embed the imagery thoughtfully in the three dimensional surfaces that create the spatial configuration of the space and place.


This research work started five years ago with the project A Room for London, (see under Projects), an installation we were investigating architecture as a three dimensional canvas to be inhabited and experienced, exploring light, shadows, spatial depth and beauty as the ingredients that might help to stir an emotional experience in the beholder; aspiring to induce human wellbeing in architecture. The imagery - both its aesthetics and content  - was an integral part of the overall deign intention. The artist we collaborated with then was Dee Ferris.


The photography embedded in the Bologna Shoah Memorial, is more three dimensional on all surfaces, and on the scroll-wall, the epic photography affects each side of the surface differently, like a haunting ghost, thus the narrative further interact with the viewer.


In this project, the selected epic photography is by the fine art photographer Ori Gersht, whose compelling stills and videos, captures beauty and violence in the same work, raising its charged, which is equally augmented by his laborious, perfectionist treatment and focused attention.


Some of his narratives portray specific associations of wars and conflicts, often transpired through their depicted locations or refugees, while others are arresting due to a violent fluctuation between figurative and abstract; a whole body of work exceptionally beautiful.


Gersht work embedded in the memorial installation’s metallic surfaces is including:

  1. 1.On the front facade of the ‘scroll wall’: Judith, stills from the video: Yehudith Arnon - Will You Dance For Me? along archival photos of deportees under the Nazi occupation.

  2. 2.On the inner facade of the ‘scroll-wall‘ - selected from the epic photography Chasing Good Fortune, a still photo entitled Floating Petals-Black Water. But Judith face, re-appears like a ghost, through the petals and the black water.

  3. 3.On each side of the ‘wing-wall’ - stills selected from the video On Reflection - creating two very different frescos; different appearances, different contents. as explained on the text above describing the project; we re-named each fresco: Shuttered Mirror and Blooming.
