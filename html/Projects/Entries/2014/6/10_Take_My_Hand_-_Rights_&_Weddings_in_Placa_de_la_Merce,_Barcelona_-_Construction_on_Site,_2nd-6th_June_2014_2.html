Take My Hand - Rights & Weddings, Placa de la Merce, Barcelona - 10th June to 11th September 2014

Plaça de la Merce - June 2104 - © Marcella Grassi

A Celebration of Rights & Weddings


The installation consists of two main parts, the inflatable canopy, and the platform flanked with fragrant flowers.

The inflatable canopy, in the form and colour of the human rights’ new universal logo – was established as a symbol to remind the world of fundamental rights in any democratic and civilised society. It was installed to celebrate human rights through civil weddings. The option of a civil marriage in many countries opened new possibilities for interfaith marriages, non-religious marriages, and same sex marriages. The inflatable, as an object in the tradition of street parades, is augmented by its practical role of projecting a shadow, which moves  daily along the square as the sun moves in the sky.

The wood platform is dedicated for the popular walk up and down the aisle - along the elevating and memorable fragrant flowers -  leading to the stage, for speeches, music playing, or the marriage registration itself. Thus, the ritual of the civil wedding, celebrating the freedom of individual choice, is spatially defined by the 'Hand' suspended above the fragrant walk.
